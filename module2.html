Bicol Express Recipe and Procedures

Bicol Express is a popular Filipino dish originating from the Bicol region. It's a spicy and savory stew made with pork, shrimp, and coconut milk, giving it a creamy and rich flavor. Here's a simple recipe to make Bicol Express:

Ingredients:

  • 500g pork belly, sliced into thin strips or cubes
  • 1/2 cup shrimp (optional, you can skip or add more if you like)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3-4 long green chilies (siling pang-sigang or siling haba), sliced diagonally
  • 2-3 red chilies (siling labuyo), chopped (adjust to your spice tolerance)
  • 1 tablespoon shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup coconut cream (optional for extra richness)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce (optional, for added depth)
  • 1/2 cup water or broth (if needed)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients:
    • Slice the pork belly into strips or small cubes.
    • If using shrimp, peel and devein them.
    • Slice the green chilies and chop the red chilies, adjusting the amount based on how spicy you want it.
  2. Cook the pork:
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat.
    • Add the pork belly and cook, stirring occasionally, until it turns golden brown and slightly crispy. This should take about 5-8 minutes.
  3. Sauté aromatics:
    • Add the chopped onions and garlic to the pan with the pork. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until they become fragrant and soft.
  4. Add shirmp paste and chilies:
    • Add the shrimp paste (bagoong) to the pan and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring well. This will help the flavors develop.
    • Add the sliced green chilies and red chilies. Stir and let the mixture cook for about 1 minute.
  5. Add coconut milk:
    • Pour in the coconut milk and bring to a simmer. Stir everything to combine.
    • Let it simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the pork becomes tender and the coconut milk thickens slightly.
  6. Add shrimp and coconut cream (optional):
    • If you're adding shrimp, add them to the pan at this point and cook for about 2-3 minutes until they turn pink and are cooked through.
    • If you want the dish to be extra creamy, add the coconut cream and mix well.
  7. Season to taste:
    • Add salt, pepper, and fish sauce (if using) to taste. Adjust the seasoning as necessary.
  8. Simmer:
    • Let the Bicol Express simmer for another 5-10 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to meld together. If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add a little water or broth to reach your desired consistency.
  9. Serve:
    • Once everything is cooked and well-seasoned, serve hot with steamed rice.
